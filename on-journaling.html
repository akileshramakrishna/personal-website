On Journaling

The practical purpose of journaling is to capture the thoughts and emotions of our day. The ethical purpose to reflect on how the events have moved us and formed our thinking. The political purpose is to inform how we would like to live in the world. The spiritual purpose is to be truly with ourselves and express our spirit in its truest sense — for where else are we fully able to outside those sacred pages, the inner sanctum for our thoughts, the loneliest, the weakest, the strongest, the fearless.

Where the beauty and richness of our lives comes to fruition. Not running to this thing or the next, but immersing in the here and now, the pen on paper. When we at times fail to make five minutes how will we ever truly live the rest in accordance to what we believe and without hurry?

To consummate the holy, the vital, the divine around us. In the shape of muses on the light at dusk, the fox at dawn, the moon and the stars. Without gratitude there can be no abundance.
